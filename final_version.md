# Generative AI for Global Health Data

I'm MJ, a first-year MPH/MBA candidate. My years in HIV immunology research gave me a firsthand appreciation for how critical clean, connected data is to understanding disease — and how much is lost when it isn't.What draws me to global health data is a deeper passion: building and improving systems that can genuinely move the needle on human wellbeing at scale.


Global health data is deeply fragmented — collected across governments, NGOs, research institutions, and health systems that rarely communicate with one another. These sources use different formats, standards, and definitions, making it difficult to get a coherent picture of population health. The data exists in abundance, but without systems that connect and interpret it, it remains invisible to the people who need it most.


Generative AI could meaningfully address these challenges in several ways:

- **Making disparate data sources interoperable** — AI can bridge incompatible formats, terminologies, and schemas, enabling data from different systems to be combined and compared.
- **Adding an intelligence layer** — Rather than leaving analysts to interpret raw numbers alone, AI can help users understand what the data is actually saying, flagging trends, anomalies, and contextual meaning.
- **Automating routine tasks** — Cleaning data, generating reports, and producing visualizations are time-consuming but necessary steps; AI can handle these so analysts can focus on higher-level analysis and decision-making.
