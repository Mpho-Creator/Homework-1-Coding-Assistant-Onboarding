# Generative AI for Global Health Data

I'm MJ, a first-year MPH/MBA candidate with a background in immunology research. I'm interested in how emerging technologies can be applied to solve complex problems in global health systems and data infrastructure.

Global health data is deeply fragmented — collected across governments, NGOs, research institutions, and health systems that rarely communicate with one another. These sources use different formats, standards, and definitions, making it difficult to get a coherent picture of population health. The data itself also lacks built-in intelligence: it exists in silos with little capacity to surface insights or guide decision-making on its own.

Generative AI could meaningfully address these challenges in several ways:

- **Making disparate data sources interoperable** — AI can bridge incompatible formats, terminologies, and schemas, enabling data from different systems to be combined and compared.
- **Adding an intelligence layer** — Rather than leaving analysts to interpret raw numbers alone, AI can help users understand what the data is actually saying, flagging trends, anomalies, and contextual meaning.
- **Automating routine tasks** — Cleaning data, generating reports, and producing visualizations are time-consuming but necessary steps; AI can handle these so analysts can focus on higher-level analysis and decision-making.
